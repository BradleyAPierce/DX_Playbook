Mapping Digital Maturity -
Levels and Dimensions
DIRECTIONS: The following exercise is based on completing the Digital Maturity module and is designed to determine your comfort level in completing an assessment with a client. This exercise may be completed either individually, or in teams. Be sure to debrief with your teammate or your instructor after completing each part ofthe exercise.

Part 1
  1. Read the customer profile.
  2. Describe the goals and benefits of completing a Digital Maturity Assessment to your client.
Part 2

  1. Based on the information provided to you, drag each Current State Dimension - Technology, Strategy, Operations, Customer - to the level that best describes the current digital maturity level of the client.
  2. Use the Dimension Priority scale at the bottom of the graph to describe the organization's priority in addressing each dimension.
    1. Example: The current digital maturity level of my client's technology is at Level 2. The priority in addressing the need to improve our IT infrastructure is High.
  3. Repeat the steps above for each dimension.
  4. Your client is eager to hear your assessment. Answer each question below as if you were speaking directly to the client. Be prepared to present your findings.
    1. What is the benefit of assessing Digital Maturity?
    2. How would you describe the client's current state of digital maturity?
    3. Explain how you arrived at each dimension rating. When possible, provide examples.
    4. Which dimension is the highest priority? Why?
    5. Which dimension represents the next highest priority? Why?
    6. Describe the impact of addressing Digital Maturity to the business? Be specific.
    7. Your client does not understand why they should consider the Organization and Culture Dimension. Explain why this dimension is important to address.
    8. What additional information would be helpful to know about this client?
    9. What would you propose as next steps?
