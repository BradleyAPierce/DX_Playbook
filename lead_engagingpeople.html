The Engaging People Journey

When you Engage People, you share company vision and invite them to participate in purposeful goals. Confidence, autonomy and complex problem solving develops and flourishes, ultimately benefiting the entire organization. The journey will look something like this:

Starting Your Engaging People Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You will actively listen to others, and ask them to share their opinions and insights about their work. You will ask questions to explore what motivates each team member to drive individual performance. You will express your appreciation for your colleagues by recognizing their contributions to the team.

Continuing Your Engaging People Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You will build strong relationships with team members by fostering the open exchange of ideas and opinions about projects. You will support your team in meeting goals and objectives by sharing what successful performance looks like. You will empower others to make decisions and create innovative solutions to problems.

Engaging People for High Impact


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You will be a role model for open and honest communication and influence your colleagues and team members to display this core value in their routine work relationships. You will empower team members and direct reports by encouraging them to work out problems and solutions to challenges themselves instead of relying on you for answers. You will instill confidence in others by recognizing their strengths and encouraging autonomy and independent decision making. You will inspire others to execute company vision and strategy by modeling successful and inclusive behavior.

Think About This: Which statement best reflects your stage in the Engaging People journey?

Statement 1: I actively listen to my team members so that I may understand their opinions and what motivates them to achieve.

Statement 2: I support my team in meeting goals, solving problems and making decisions by demonstrating successful performance.

Statement 3: I inspire others to execute company vision and strategy by role modeling successful behavior.
