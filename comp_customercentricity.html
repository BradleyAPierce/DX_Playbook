The Customer Centricity Journey

As you develop Customer Centric thinking, your behavior and skill set also develop in new ways. The journey will look something like this:

Starting your Customer Centric Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You will begin by establishing trustworthy relationships with your external or internal customers. You will ask questions and actively listen so that you can understand your customers' needs and issues. You will focus on ensuring a good customer experience in your daily work.

Continuing Your Customer Centric Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You will gain understanding of the customers' short term and long term needs and goals, and take them into consideration in all aspects of your work. You will seek feedback from your customers to uncover ways to provide them with better service. You will help your entire team, and the larger Konica Minolta organization, deliver greater value to all customers.

Developing Customer Centricity for High Impact


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You will be a role model for other employees by demonstrating and sharing insights about the best practices to enhance the customer experience. Based on your deep understanding of customer requirements, you will seize opportunities to innovate in ways that enhance the customer experience. You will empower other employees by sharing success stories and other resources that provide ideas and inspiration for supporting customers.

Think About This: Which statement best reflects your stage in the Customer Centric Journey?

Statement 2: I actively listen to other employees talking about customer success stories to find lessons I can apply in my work.

Statement 3: I always seek feedback from my internal / external customers so that I can improve my services to support them.
