Embracing all of the ways in which we are unique to increase engagement and generate new ideas.

Why is Diversity, Equity, & Inclusion (DEI) important to our work?

Research shows many benefits of a diverse, equitable, and inclusive workplace. These include:
• Higher revenue growth
• Greater readiness to innovate
• Increased ability to recruit a diverse talent pool
• Higher employee retention
- "Getting Serious About Diversity" Harvard Business Review Nov/Dec 2020

What's in it for....

Now that you have a better understanding of what DEI looks like in the workplace, let's take a look at the benefits.

You?

You?

  • Continuous learning about Diversity, Equity and Inclusion increases your knowledge about your colleagues as well as your appreciation of the world around you.
  • Your sense of belonging, confidence, and perspective will improve.
  • Your acceptance of diverse backgrounds and new ideas will enrich your daily work and bring value to the company culture.
  • Your communication and behavior will demonstrate a deeper level of compassion and acceptance of all individuals regardless of background.
  • Strong DEI initiatives help prevent workplace burnout, whether employees work remotely, hybrid, or in-person.

Your team?

Your team?

  • Your pool of team members and prospective applicants will increase, becoming more diverse, offering new perspectives and increasing innovative thinking.

The Diversity, Equity & Inclusion Journey

Understanding and fully embracing DEI as integral to our beliefs and behavior commences with the acceptance, demonstration, and respect for individual and group differences.
The journey will look something like this:

Starting Your DEI Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You listen to the opinions, ideas and concerns of others without bias. You examine your own thoughts and assumptions when challenged, and adapt your behavior and way of working as needed. You understand and support opportunities for recognizing and integrating diversity within the workplace.

Continuing Your DEI Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You pursue opportunities to engage others who have skills, beliefs and experiences that are different from your own. You perceive individual and cultural differences as valuable and enriching to projects and company goals. You leverage the diverse nature of our workforce and utilize employee talents to generate new ideas and craft the best solutions for our customers. You recognize potential inequities and take accountability for avoiding any potential opportunity for exclusion. You celebrate diversity and actively participate in opportunities to improve acceptance.

Developing DEI for High Impact


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

You ensure that diversity is incorporated into programs and services within the broader organization. You proactively address existing or proposed processes that could lead to inequities and eradicate them. You regularly engage and support workplace dialogue around diversity topics to reinforce positive employee values and associated inclusive behaviors. You nurture an environment that fosters diversity by promoting the benefits of embracing individual differences and supporting contributions from all individuals. You foster an inclusive workplace that maximizes the talents of each person to achieve positive business results.

Think About This: Which statement reflects your stage in the Diversity, Equity & Inclusion journey?

Statement 1: I understand and support opportunities for recognizing and integrating diversity within the workplace.

Statement 2: I pursue opportunities to engage others who have skills, beliefs and experiences that are different from my own.

Statement 3: I recognize potential inequities and take accountability for avoiding any potential opportunity for exclusion.

Statement 4: I foster an inclusive workplace that maximizes the talents of each person to achieve positive business results within an environment of safety and acceptance.
