Why IIM?


Click the play button to listen to the audio or scroll through the text below.



Digital Transformation challenges all organizations to use data to extract business intelligence that will ultimately fuel business strategy execution. Data insights allow smarter products and services to be created, business processes to be improved and for company talent to be utilized more effectively. And while there is no shortage of data, the data we do have is typically unstructured, unorganized, inaccessible and vulnerable to external threats. In other words, it is often unusable.

Konica Minolta's IIM Practice supports customers with a comprehensive solution to digitize, organize and analyze unstructured data, automate business processes, increase collaboration and save time and money, all in a safe and secure environment. The combination of software-as-a-service product offerings and expert professional services extracts business intelligence to improve business performance. And that's what DX is all about. Best of all, Konica Minolta's IIM professionals are prepared to meet our customers wherever they are in their DX journey and guide them to accomplishing business goals. How does it all happen? How does IIM help our customers transform?


Click the play button to listen to the audio or scroll through the text below.



Organizations are brimming with data. Think about it. Even small to midsize organizations have loads of information that can become unmanageable very quickly, employee data, customer data, financial data, inventory. And that's not the only challenge. Is the information paper-based? Where is it stored? How quickly can it be accessed?

As the amount of information grows, individual departments attempt to organize it in a meaningful way, alphabetically by date, by project name, by location. In time, the “meaningful” filing structure becomes archaic and unusable. Employees spend their time looking for information instead of helping customers, problem solving or advancing the business.

How can Konica Minolta help? Digitizing information is a great first step, but it's just the beginning. Organizing information according to need, analyzing data, evaluating and automating business processes, and gathering the right intelligence are also critical to ensuring that business operations are running as efficiently as possible.
