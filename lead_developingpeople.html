Now that you have a better understanding of what it means to Develop People, let's take a look at the benefits. What's In it for you, your team and Konica Minolta?

You?

  • By spending time with each member of my team and helping them to recognize their strengths and skills, I am activating an individual development plan that serves as a guide to professional development.

The Developing People Journey

As you Develop People, you establish a foundation for future growth within the organization. When individuals and teams are encouraged and empowered to develop their skills, their mindset and behaviors evolve in positive ways. New ideas are generated, business improves and the corporate culture flourishes. The journey will look something like this:

Starting Your Developing People Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

Before assuming the responsibility of developing others, you evaluate your own strengths and weaknesses and actively engage in learning opportunities that will build skills. You approach individual team members in the same manner as you connect individual development paths with available learning and expose them to continued development. Throughout, you provide constructive feedback.

Continuing Your Developing People Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

Now that you understand individual strengths and weaknesses, you focus your attention on the collective capabilities of the team. You support others in building their development plans and evaluate the progress together. You explore growth opportunities for team members such as stretch assignments, job crafting, job rotation and delegation opportunities. You design and set challenging goals for team members and guide them to successful completion.

Developing People for High Impact


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

As your team develops, your constructive feedback begins to drive improved problem solving and idea generation. The team makes bolder decisions and assumes increasing levels of responsibility. Individual Development plans are realized and recognized, and the corporate culture flourishes. You lead with dedication to people development by demonstrating commitment to the success of others, including those who are underrepresented, within an environment of psychological safety and trust.

Think About This: Which statement best reflects your stage in the Developing People journey?

Statement 1: I have a very good understanding of my own strengths and weaknesses and am beginning to understand the unique skills, capabilities and career goals of the individuals on my team.

Statement 2: I am focused on building Individual Development Plans for my team. I actively provide feedback and continuously look for assignments and opportunities that foster growth and development.

Statement 3: As I lead my team, I engage them in projects that offer autonomy, creativity and bold decision making. I assist each team member in realizing their career aspirations and provide recognition for accomplishments.
