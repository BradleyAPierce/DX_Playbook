The Conceptual Thinking Journey

Conceptual Thinking facilitates an understanding of how events, people and processes impact the organization on a global scale. It also allows broad-based problem solving and idea creation to take place so that true impact is realized. The journey looks something like this:

Starting Your Conceptual Thinking Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

As your ability to think conceptually matures, you understand how the different parts of the organization interrelate with one another and how that dynamic impacts the business. You instinctively evaluate business issues by extracting patterns, similarities and differences within the context of the "bigger picture". You collect relevant data and information and then apply their insights to different business situations with the goal of solving problems and improving existing processes.

Continuing Your Conceptual Thinking Journey


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

As you progress, you consult with a variety of sources across the organization to develop innovative solutions to core problems that have global impact. You actively explore complex situations, capturing and analyzing information from different areas of the organization to formulate holistic assessments of business challenges. You focus on improving business scenarios with innovative and creative suggestions that resolve core challenges. You invite and involve others to explore new ideas with the focus on providing sustainable solutions that resolve core challenges.

Developing Conceptual Thinking for High Impact


Click the play button to listen to the audio.

Your ability to think systematically and understand highly complex business scenarios that impact multiple areas of the organization is instinctive to you. You embrace the interdependencies of different projects and initiatives and foster their integration across the wider organization. You continuously seek out new ideas with a focus on innovation and improvement as you connect different concepts and contrasting ideas to improve business scenarios and resolve core challenges. You are recognized as an ambidextrous thought leader who is able to simultaneously optimize existing business models while searching for innovative ones.

Statement 2: I actively explore complex situations, capturing and analyzing information from different areas of the organization to formulate holistic assessments of business challenges.

Statement 3: I embrace the interdependencies of different projects and initiatives and foster their integration across the wider organization.
